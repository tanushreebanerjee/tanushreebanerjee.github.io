# NeRF Appearance & Relighting **Module 5.3** | [← Back to Index](../index.html) | [Previous: NeRF Acceleration ←](05-nerf-acceleration.md.html) --- ## Overview NeRF appearance modeling: Decompose view-dependent appearance into shape, reflectance, and lighting. Enables relighting and material editing. --- ## Essential Papers ### NeRFactor (2021) **NeRFactor: Neural Factorization of Shape and Reflectance under an Unknown Illumination** (Bi et al.) | [arXiv](https://arxiv.org/abs/2006.04970) **Key idea**: Factorize NeRF into geometry, BRDF (material), and lighting. **Pipeline**: 1. Train NeRF to learn geometry and appearance 2. Extract surface geometry (marching cubes) 3. Optimize BRDF properties (albedo, roughness, normal) 4. Estimate lighting environment **Output**: Relightable scene with material properties. [Figure placeholder: NeRFactor factorization diagram] --- ### Ref-NeRF (2022) **Ref-NeRF: Structured View-Dependent Appearance for Neural Radiance Fields** (Verbin et al.) | [arXiv](https://arxiv.org/abs/2112.03907) **Key innovation**: Explicitly model view-dependent appearance using learned normal and reflection direction. **Appearance model**: Separates diffuse and specular components. **Benefits**: Better view-dependent modeling, enables relighting. --- ### Neural Reflectance Fields **Goal**: Decompose appearance into material properties. **Components**: Albedo, roughness, metallic, normal maps. **Applications**: Material editing, relighting, realistic rendering. --- ## Core Concepts ### Appearance Factorization **Geometry**: 3D shape (surface, volume). **Material**: BRDF properties (albedo, roughness, metallic). **Lighting**: Environment maps, light sources. **Combined**: Render realistic appearance under different lighting. --- ### View-Dependent Appearance **Challenge**: NeRF learns view-dependent color but doesn't separate causes. **Solution**: Explicitly model reflection direction, normal, lighting. **Benefits**: Better understanding, enables editing. --- ## Problems Solved by Appearance Modeling ### Enabling Relighting **Problem**: Standard NeRF learns appearance under training lighting. Cannot change lighting or relight scene. **Appearance factorization solution**: Decompose appearance into geometry, materials, and lighting. Enables relighting by changing lighting component. [Figure placeholder: Visualization showing NeRF appearance factorization: geometry + material + lighting = appearance, enabling relighting] ### Material Editing **Problem**: Standard NeRF appearance cannot be edited. Cannot change material properties (make object more shiny, change color, etc.). **Solution**: Explicit material properties (BRDF) enable editing. Change material properties, re-render with new appearance. ### Understanding View-Dependent Effects **Problem**: NeRF learns view-dependent appearance but doesn't separate causes (geometry, materials, lighting). **Ref-NeRF solution**: Explicitly models reflection direction, normals, lighting. Better understanding of view-dependent appearance. --- ## Remaining Challenges and Limitations ### Ambiguity in Factorization **Problem**: Factorization is ambiguous - same appearance can result from different combinations of geometry, materials, lighting. **Open question**: Better constraints to resolve ambiguity? Additional supervision? ### Quality of Material Estimation **Problem**: Estimated material properties may not be physically accurate or realistic. **Remaining**: Challenging to estimate accurate BRDF parameters from limited views. ### Complex Lighting **Problem**: Very complex lighting (many lights, area lights, environment maps) can be difficult to model accurately. **Open question**: Better lighting representation? More expressive lighting models? ### Generalization **Problem**: Material estimation may not generalize well across different scene types or lighting conditions. **Open question**: Better generalization? More robust material estimation? --- ## Broader Insights and Implications ### The Ambiguity Problem in Inverse Rendering **Insight**: Appearance factorization highlights fundamental ambiguity - same appearance can result from different factor combinations. **Broader impact**: Demonstrates importance of priors and constraints in inverse problems. Need additional information to resolve ambiguity. Influences design of inverse rendering methods. ### Physically-Based vs Learned **Insight**: Physically-based material models (BRDF) provide interpretability and editing capabilities, but learned representations may be more flexible. **Broader impact**: Trade-off between interpretability (physics) and flexibility (learning). Different applications need different approaches. ### Enabling New Applications **Insight**: Appearance modeling enables new applications (relighting, material editing, product visualization) not possible with standard NeRF. **Broader impact**: Shows that decomposition enables new capabilities. Influences design of neural rendering methods.
